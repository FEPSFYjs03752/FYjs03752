Abstract

This articles simply summarises the fundamental differences between High intensity Training vs low intensity training with its respect to Cardiovascular health, fat loss, muscle endurance and recovery. Presentation:

High Itensity Taining(HIT):

HIT method requires short quick bursts of intense training done at 70-90% of one’s maximum effort capabilities, a good example of such can be high intensity interval training (HIIT) which includes; sprinting, CrossFit, plyometrics and circuit training. One of the greatest advantages of HIT is that it is incredibly time efficient, requiring only 20-30 minutes per day and offering substantial health benefits in terms of calorie expenditure, “enhancing cardiovascular function” [Little, J.P. et al. (2010,p. 2-7)] , “Increasing VO2 max (maximum oxygen uptake)” [Scribbans et al,(2016)] and “Enhanced metabolic efficiency of muscle tissue”.

The excess post exercise oxygen consumption or EPOC. Which referrers to the body continuing to “impact fuel utilization for up to 24 hours post-exercise” [Collins et al,(2020)] as oxygen levels and muscle tissue is regulated while metabolic waste is removed. This makes the training highly “superior in reducing total body fat when compared with moderate-intensity continuous training” [Panissa et al,(2020)] ; ideal for individuals whom wish to maximise fat loss in the shortest period of time.

HIT also increases the VO2 max of a person which enables them to preform better in activities that require quick releases of energy, such as weightlifting and or competitive sports. Proof of this can be seen on the graph below of 61 participants which shows that high intensity interval training (HIIT) leads to “greater improvements in VO2 max compared to moderate-intensity continuous training”. [Gromley et al,(2008)]

The intensity of the workouts however raise the likely hood of injury especially among beginners or people with previous conditions. Common injury’s include muscle strains, joint problems and cardiovascular stress. So the proper warm ups and cool downs / adequate recovery are essential to avoid overtraining, else a sharp decline in performance and increased risk of injury occur. However when done correctly there can be “rapid muscle growth and strength increases”[benito et al,(2020] with in the first year of training as well as “Quick improvements in neuromuscular coordination” [benito et al,(2020)], this is commonly known as newbie gains.

Low Intensity Training(LIT)

LIT requires Prolonged and steady paced activities which are preformed at around 50-65% of one’s innate athletic abilities. Different exercises would include walking, lightly jogging, yoga and swimming. LIT concentrates on duration of the exercise rather than intensity, hence it can be carried out by a larger range of individuals such as beginners or the elderly.

One of the most crucial benefits of LIT is the larger impact on the cardiovascular system, “regular prolonged activity can improve the body’s blood circulation” [T meyer, Auracher Heeg,(2007)] ,reduce the "blood pressure and Improve heart function”[Eijsvogels et al,(2016)] over time, which lowers the risk of heart disease and strokes. Moreover as the body mainly utilises aerobic respiration, it burns the fat stores primarily for energy with out depleting muscle glycogen reserves. Making it very effective at weight loss. More on this can be seen in the graph below.

LIT has less physically demanding requirements, meaning people of all ages can partake in the activities without risk of burnout / injury for a sustained period, this “offers both physical and cognitive health benefits”. Promoting mental clarity and stress relief.

Finally, however LIT may not be the best choice for anyone looking to increase muscle mass significantly, as the minimal effort does not adequately activate all of the muscle fibres resulting in low amouts of hypertrophy, whcih intern results in minimal muscle gain; thus results in low metabloic change. These short comings however can be made up as LIT does improve mind muscle connections, this allows more muscle fibres to be activated when caled upon for strenuous tasks, allowing for more peak performance of a muscle with less effort required.
